"By integrating the functions of mind, body, and breath in the same time frame, a practitioner will experience the real joy of yoga practice... The mind closely follows the slow, smooth, deliberate ujjayi yogic breathing; and the yoking of mind and body takes place with the breath acting as a harness."—Srivatsa Ramaswami, The Complete Book of Vinyasa Yoga

Introduction

Principles

  • Expansive movements (backbends, moving limbs away from the torso) are done on inhalation.
  • Contractive movements (forward bends, bringing limbs back to the core) are done on exhalation.
  • Twists are done on exhalation.

Benefits

This set can be practiced daily. It will help you relax and get in touch with your body. I highly recommend it for older students and for people with back pain.

Props

Yoga mat. A blanket under your head if your chin juts out while lying on your back and a meditation cushion/blanket if you can't sit upright in a cross-legged position.
